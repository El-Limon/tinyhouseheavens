**Problem:** Purely aspirational/marketing copy. No factual content. No definition pattern. Not quotable by AI -- an AI answering "What is Tiny House Heavens?" cannot cite a tagline.

**Suggested rewrite:**
> "Tiny House Heavens is a Belgian modular tiny house manufacturer based in Sint-Katelijne-Waver, offering 20 unique models ranging from 13m2 studios to 112m2 family homes. All models feature steel undercarriage construction with timber frames and 150-200mm insulation, available in three finishing levels from self-build kits (EUR 25,000+) to turnkey homes (EUR 200,000+). The company serves Belgium, the Netherlands, and Luxembourg."

**Additional improvements:**
- Add a "Who we are" paragraph below the hero with company facts
- Include founding year, number of homes delivered, or team size
- Bold key terms: "modular tiny house", "Benelux", model count

### 2. Model Page: Evoke Description -- Score: 30/100

**Suggested rewrite:**

Measuring 12.0 x 4.5 metres with a gable roof, it is the company's most popular mid-range model. Available in three finishing levels: House Kit (structural shell), Basic (fully finished with installations), and Optima (turnkey with underfloor heating and ventilation system)."

**Additional improvements:**
- Move the specs table higher on the page (above gallery)
- Add a comparison with similar-sized models (table format)
- Include a starting price or price range

### 3. Homepage: "Why Tiny House Heavens?" -- Score: 35/100

**Current opening:**
> "Innovative: Modern designs with smart storage and smart home technology. Scalable: Modular systems that expand without major renovations."

**Problem:** Generic value propositions without any supporting evidence. "Modern designs" and "smart storage" are vague claims. No statistics, no comparisons, no proof points.

**Suggested rewrite:**
> "Tiny House Heavens builds modular tiny houses with four core advantages. First, smart design: each model maximises usable space with built-in storage -- the 54m2 Evoke achieves 42.6m2 of living area (79% space efficiency). Second, scalability: the modular steel-and-timber frame system allows extensions without structural renovation. Third, affordability: tiny houses cost EUR 1,200-2,500/m2 compared to EUR 2,500-3,500/m2 for traditional Belgian construction. Fourth, sustainability: 150-200mm insulation and optional Ventilation D heat recovery systems reduce energy consumption by up to 60% versus standard Belgian homes."

**Additional improvements:**
- Add a comparison table: tiny house vs traditional home (cost, energy, build time)
- Include at least one third-party statistic or source

### 4. Blog: Permit Guide Introduction -- Score: 38/100

**Current opening:**
> "Yes, in most cases you need a planning permission to place a tiny house in Belgium."

**Problem:** Good answer-first format but too brief. Needs the key follow-up facts in the same opening passage to be fully citable.

**Suggested rewrite:**
> "Yes, in most cases you need a planning permission (omgevingsvergunning in Flanders, permis d'urbanisme in Wallonia) to place a tiny house in Belgium. Belgium has no specific tiny house legislation -- instead, tiny houses fall under existing spatial planning rules that vary by region and municipality. The application process typically takes 30 to 120 days, and in Flanders the minimum net floor area for an independent dwelling is 18m2. Only tiny houses used purely for recreational purposes may qualify for simplified procedures."

## Quick Win Reformatting Recommendations

1. **Add a definition paragraph to the top of every model page** -- Each model page should open with "[Model] is a [X]m2 modular tiny house with [Y]m2 living space..." instead of marketing taglines. Expected citability lift: +12 points per model page.

2. **Add source citations to the cost blog post** -- The price ranges lack attribution. Add "Based on Tiny House Heavens' 2026 model pricing" or cite Belgian construction cost indices. Expected citability lift: +8 points.

3. **Convert the finishing levels comparison to a proper HTML table** -- Currently displayed as three separate card sections. A single comparison table (House Kit vs Basic vs Optima with rows for each feature) is more extractable by AI. Expected citability lift: +6 points.

4. **Add "last updated" dates to all blog posts and FAQ** -- AI systems weight content freshness. Visible update dates (not just publication) signal currency. Expected citability lift: +4 points.

5. **Bold key statistics and named entities on first use** -- Numbers like "20 models", "13-112m2", "EUR 25,000-200,000", and entity names like "omgevingsvergunning" should be bolded. This aids AI entity recognition during content parsing. Expected citability lift: +3 points.

6. **Restructure the homepage "How it works" section as a numbered definition list** -- Currently prose-heavy. Convert to: "Step 1: Choose your model from 20 designs (13-112m2)..." format with specific details in each step. Expected citability lift: +5 points.

7. **Add a "Key facts" summary box to each blog post** -- A highlighted box at the top with 4-5 bullet points containing the core facts. This creates an immediately extractable passage. Expected citability lift: +10 points.
