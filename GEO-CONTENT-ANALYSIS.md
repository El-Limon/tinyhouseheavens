## Content Quality Issues

### 1. Anonymous Authorship (Critical)
Every blog post lists "Tiny House Heavens" as author. No individual bylines, no author bios, no Person schema. This is the single biggest E-E-A-T deficit. Google's quality raters specifically look for identifiable content creators.

**Example:** The cost blog post makes claims about price ranges (EUR 25,000-200,000) with no attributed source -- neither an internal expert nor an external reference.

### 2. No External Sources Cited (High)
Across all 4 blog posts and the FAQ page, zero external sources are cited. The permits guide discusses Belgian regional regulations without linking to a single government website. The cost guide gives price ranges without citing construction cost indices or market data.

**Recommended sources to add:**
- Flanders permit portal: omgevingsloketvlaanderen.be
- Belgian energy performance: energiesparen.be
- Construction cost data: Statbel (Belgian statistics office)

### 3. No Case Studies or Customer Evidence (High)
The site claims to build tiny houses but provides zero evidence of completed projects. No customer testimonials, no project photos with context, no "delivered in [location]" stories. This makes all expertise claims unverifiable.

### 4. Bulk-Published Content (Medium)
All 4 blog posts share the same publication date (April 10, 2026). A natural publishing cadence would spread these over weeks or months. Simultaneous publication suggests launch content rather than an established knowledge base.

### 5. About Page Lacks Human Identity (Medium)
The About page (~500 words) describes the company's mission and values but names no individuals. No founder, no head of construction, no design lead. The page functions as a company brochure, not an authority-building asset.
