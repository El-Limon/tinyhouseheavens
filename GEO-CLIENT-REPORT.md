## Executive Summary

We analyzed tinyhouseheavens.com across 30+ pages in three languages (Dutch, French, English), assessing AI search readiness across Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and Bing Copilot. Tiny House Heavens scores **39/100** on our GEO Readiness Index, placing it in the "Needs Attention" tier. The site has an excellent technical foundation — fast-loading static HTML, all AI crawlers explicitly allowed, and solid structured data — but is held back by near-zero brand presence outside its own domain, no named authors or customer testimonials, and no visibility on the platforms AI systems rely on for citations (YouTube, Reddit, Wikipedia, LinkedIn). The three highest-priority actions are: (1) claim a Google Business Profile, (2) add a named founder with credentials to the site, and (3) begin building external platform presence starting with LinkedIn and YouTube. Addressing the full action plan outlined in this report could increase AI-driven discovery by an estimated 40-60%, representing significant new lead generation for a business in the EUR 25,000-200,000 price range.

## Citability Analysis

### Top 5 Most Citable Pages

**1. FAQ Page** (/en/faq/) — Citability: High
Your FAQ page with 8 structured Q&A pairs and FAQPage schema is the single most citable asset on the site. The definition of "modular tiny house" and the energy efficiency answer contain specific technical facts that AI systems can extract verbatim.
- *To improve:* Add 4-5 more questions covering topics AI is frequently asked (cost ranges, permit requirements, build timelines).

**2. Blog: Tiny House Costs in Belgium** (/en/blog/how-much-does-a-tiny-house-cost-in-belgium/)
The price comparison table (EUR 25,000-200,000 by size category) is highly extractable. This page answers one of the most common AI queries about Belgian tiny houses.
- *To improve:* Add external source citations for the price data and a "Key Facts" summary box at the top.

**3. Blog: Permits Complete Guide** (/en/blog/tiny-house-permits-belgium-complete-guide/)
Region-specific permit information (Flanders omgevingsvergunning, Wallonia permis d'urbanisme) with specific requirements and timelines is exactly what AI systems need for regulatory queries.
- *To improve:* Link to official government permit portals as sources.

**4. Blog: Buying Guide** (/en/blog/buying-a-tiny-house-in-belgium-guide/)
The structured 8-step guide with modular vs. on-wheels comparison table provides clear, extractable comparison content.
- *To improve:* Add author credentials and cited sources for builder selection criteria.

**5. Model Page: Evoke** (/en/models/evoke/)
Detailed specifications with Product schema provide structured data AI systems can reference for product comparison queries.
- *To improve:* Open with a factual definition sentence instead of the marketing tagline.

### Top 5 Least Citable Pages

**1. About Us** (/en/about-us/) — Citability: Low
No named individuals, no founding story, no credentials, no project evidence. An AI answering "Who is Tiny House Heavens?" has only generic mission statements to cite.
- *Action:* Add founder name, photo, bio with credentials, and Person schema.

**2. Services Page** (/en/services/) — Citability: Low
Generic service descriptions without timelines, pricing, guarantees, or case studies. An AI cannot cite any specific service commitment.
- *Action:* Add specific details: "Design consultation typically takes 2-3 weeks", "Delivery within 4-12 weeks depending on finishing level."

**3. Model Listing Page** (/en/models/) — Citability: Low
A grid of cards with model names and sizes. No narrative content for AI to cite.
- *Action:* Add an introductory paragraph explaining the model range with key comparison data.

**4. Contact Page** (/en/contact/) — Citability: Low
Standard form page. Missing Google Maps embed and business hours.
- *Action:* Add business hours and Google Maps embed for local search signals.

**5. Homepage Hero Section** — Citability: Very Low
"Simple living, great thinking" is a tagline, not citable content. The hero section is the first thing AI crawlers read but contains zero factual information about the business.
- *Action:* Add a factual paragraph below the hero: "Tiny House Heavens is a Belgian manufacturer of modular tiny houses, offering 20 models from 13m2 to 112m2..."

Your most citable pages are your best candidates for appearing in AI-generated answers. Improving the 5 least citable pages represents the highest-ROI content investment you can make for AI visibility.

### Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| GEO | Generative Engine Optimization — optimizing content to be cited by AI search platforms |
| AIO | AI Overviews — Google's AI-generated answer boxes at the top of search results |
| E-E-A-T | Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness — Google's content quality framework |
| SSR/SSG | Server-Side Rendering / Static Site Generation — generating HTML on the server so crawlers can read content without JavaScript |
| JSON-LD | JavaScript Object Notation for Linked Data — preferred structured data format for search engines and AI |
| sameAs | Schema.org property linking an entity to its profiles on other platforms |
| IndexNow | Protocol for instantly notifying Bing (and ChatGPT/Copilot) of new content |
| llms.txt | Emerging standard file that guides AI systems about a site's content and structure |
| FAQPage | Schema.org type for structured Q&A content that AI systems extract for direct answers |
| Person schema | Structured data identifying a specific individual with credentials — critical for E-E-A-T |
